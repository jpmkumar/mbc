\begin{abstract}
Breast cancer diagnosis relies on heterogeneous imaging modalities---mammography, ultrasound, and thermography---yet most deep learning systems require paired multimodal datasets that are scarce in clinical practice. We propose a Modality-Level Generalized Hybrid Quantum Framework that learns unified benign/malignant patterns across modalities without patient-level multimodal pairing. The pipeline combines modality token embedding, EfficientNet-B0 feature extraction, a Transformer encoder, compression to eight dimensions, and a two-stage hybrid classifier: a classical head (Stage~A) followed by an optional variational quantum circuit (VQC) head (Stage~B) with angle encoding and hardware-efficient entanglement~\cite{benedetti2019vqc}. On CBIS-DDSM mammography ($n=2{,}966$ ROI images; test $n=445$), enhanced preprocessing (CLAHE, grayscale) and class-weighted Stage~A training achieve \textbf{70.1\% balanced accuracy}, \textbf{84.7\% malignant recall}, and \textbf{0.763 AUC}---outperforming the initial pipeline (61.8\%) and Benedetti-aligned VQC Stage~B (69.0\%). A prior tabular EQML pilot on the Wisconsin Breast Cancer Diagnostic dataset reached $\sim$97\% SVM accuracy, highlighting the greater difficulty of mammography ROI classification. Multi-method explainability (Grad-CAM, attention, SHAP) supports clinical interpretability. Code and reproduction scripts are publicly available.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\IEEEPARstart{B}{reast} cancer remains a leading cause of cancer mortality worldwide. Diagnostic imaging spans mammography, ultrasound, and thermography, but hospital workflows often provide only one modality per patient. Deep learning models typically excel within a single modality yet fail to generalize across imaging types without expensive paired datasets.

We address this gap with a unified learning framework that: (1) accepts any single modality at inference; (2) learns modality-invariant representations via Transformer encoding with learnable modality tokens; (3) classifies via a hybrid classical--quantum pipeline with angle-encoded VQC; and (4) integrates SHAP, Grad-CAM, and attention visualization for clinical transparency.

\textbf{Contributions:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Unified cross-modality breast cancer learning without paired multimodal data
    \item Transformer-based modality-invariant representation learning with $[$MAMMO$]$, $[$ULTRA$]$, $[$THERMO$]$ tokens
    \item Two-stage hybrid training with Benedetti-aligned 8-qubit VQC (LayerNorm, full readout)~\cite{benedetti2019vqc}
    \item CBIS-DDSM mammography evaluation with enhanced preprocessing reaching 70.1\% balanced accuracy
    \item Explainable AI integration: SHAP, Grad-CAM, and Transformer attention maps
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\textbf{Breast imaging AI.} CNN and ViT models achieve strong performance on mammography (CBIS-DDSM)~\cite{lee2017cbis} and ultrasound (BUSI)~\cite{aldhabyani2020busi} but remain modality-specific.

\textbf{Cross-modality learning.} Unified encoders and domain adaptation reduce modality gap yet often assume paired or aligned data---unrealistic in low-resource settings.

\textbf{Hybrid quantum ML.} Quantum feature maps and VQCs~\cite{schuld2019quantum,havlicek2019supervised,benedetti2019vqc} enable non-linear decision boundaries in low-dimensional encodings. Transfer learning with frozen classical extractors~\cite{mari2020transfer} improves VQC trainability.

\textbf{Explainable medical AI.} Grad-CAM~\cite{selvaraju2017gradcam} and SHAP~\cite{lundberg2017shap} provide complementary global and local explanations essential for clinical adoption.

\section{Proposed Method}
Fig.~\ref{fig:arch} illustrates the pipeline. An input image from any supported modality receives a learnable modality token, passes through EfficientNet-B0~\cite{tan2019efficientnet} for local feature extraction, and enters a Transformer encoder for modality-invariant representation learning. A compression MLP ($2048 \rightarrow 128 \rightarrow 32 \rightarrow 8$) feeds either a classical linear head (Stage~A) or a VQC head (Stage~B).

\subsection{Modality Token Embedding}
Learnable embeddings $\mathbf{e}_m \in \mathbb{R}^d$ for $m \in \{\text{mammo}, \text{ultra}, \text{thermo}\}$ are prepended to projected EfficientNet features, enabling the Transformer to condition on modality while learning shared representations.

\subsection{Variational Quantum Circuit}
Following Benedetti et al.~\cite{benedetti2019vqc}, compressed features undergo LayerNorm, sigmoid angle encoding to $[0,\pi]$, and a hardware-efficient ansatz (RY/RZ rotations with linear CNOT entanglement). Full Pauli-$Z$ readout on eight qubits feeds a classical linear classifier.

\subsection{Training Strategy}
Fig.~\ref{fig:stages} summarizes the protocol from EQML pilot findings~\cite{eqml_pilot2025}: (A) classical backbone + compression with linear head; (B) frozen backbone, VQC head only; (C) optional joint fine-tuning. GPU mixed-precision accelerates Stage~A; feature caching accelerates Stage~B.

\section{Experimental Setup}
\textbf{Datasets.} Primary results use CBIS-DDSM mammography~\cite{lee2017cbis} ($n=2{,}966$ ROI images; Table~\ref{tab:dataset}). Ultrasound (BUSI) and thermography experiments use synthetic placeholders pending full dataset download; cross-modality generalization is future work.

\textbf{Preprocessing.} Enhanced Stage~A/B: grayscale conversion, CLAHE contrast enhancement ($clip=2.0$), resize to $224\times224$, ImageNet normalization. Train-time augmentation: horizontal flip, $\pm5°$ rotation.

\textbf{Baselines.} Initial Stage~A (default config); Benedetti VQC Stage~B; enhanced Stage~A/B (\texttt{mammo\_enhanced.yaml}: CLAHE, malignant class weight $\times 1.8$, 30 Stage~A epochs).

\textbf{Metrics.} Accuracy, balanced accuracy, precision, recall, F1, AUC-ROC on held-out test split ($n=445$).

\textbf{Hardware.} Google Colab T4 GPU; PyTorch + PennyLane \texttt{lightning.qubit} (adjoint); classical modules on GPU, VQC on CPU.

\section{Discussion}
The framework supports single-modality clinical workflows without paired data. On CBIS-DDSM, classical Stage~A with domain-appropriate preprocessing outperformed VQC Stage~B under simulation, suggesting quantum advantage may require larger imaging cohorts or task-specific circuit design. Thermography and ultrasound claims await real BUSI/thermo data.

\textbf{Limitations.} Simulation-only quantum evaluation; synthetic non-mammography modalities; single seed (42) for primary imaging results; ROI-level CBIS-DDSM scale ($\sim$3k) vs patch-mined corpora in large-scale literature.

\section{Conclusion}
We presented a modality-level generalized hybrid quantum framework for breast cancer classification. On CBIS-DDSM, enhanced classical Stage~A reached 70.1\% balanced accuracy with high malignant recall, establishing a reproducible baseline for future hybrid and cross-modality extensions.
